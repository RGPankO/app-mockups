Feature Breakdown

A clear map of how each screen earns its place. This is meant for stakeholders who want to understand the behavior change strategy, not just the interface.

Dashboard

Daily clarity and momentum

The dashboard pulls today's check-in, symptoms, and micro-metrics into one glance. It turns a complex, fluctuating experience into a simple "what's my day like?" moment.

It exists to reduce cognitive load and encourage daily logging. It earns the top real estate because it creates habit momentum and surfaces fast, actionable nudges.

Log Symptoms

High-fidelity symptom capture

The log screen is where users record what's happening right now, including context notes. It transforms vague discomfort into structured data.

It solves the "I can't remember patterns" problem and builds the dataset that makes insights and doctor prep credible. It deserves its screen because it's the primary input that powers everything else.

Insights

Actionable pattern recognition

Insights translate raw logs into specific correlations: triggers, recovery boosts, and time-of-day rhythms. It answers "why is this happening?"

This screen exists to build confidence and reduce guesswork. It earns space because insight-driven behavior change is the product's core value.

Doctor Prep

Shared decision support

Doctor Prep compiles a clean, shareable summary for appointments, turning months of daily logs into a concise narrative.

It solves the "I freeze in the exam room" problem and elevates the user's voice in care decisions. It deserves its screen because it is the trust-building bridge to real-world outcomes.
