Feature Breakdown

For evaluators: each core screen earns its place by compressing mental load into a single, decisive action loop. This section explains what each screen does, why it exists, and the value it returns to the user.

Brain Dump

  • What it does: Captures unstructured thoughts and turns them into prioritized, labeled tasks.
  • Why it exists: People lose tasks when everything lives in their head; this gives a single inlet for chaos.
  • Value: Instant relief plus a cleaned-up task list without the user doing the organizing.
  • Screen value: It is the highest-frequency entry point, so it deserves a full-screen, zero-friction canvas.

Today

  • What it does: Translates all tasks into a realistic daily plan with timing, load balance, and reminders.
  • Why it exists: Traditional lists ignore capacity; this prevents overload and decision fatigue.
  • Value: Gives a calm, doable plan that protects focus and energy.
  • Screen value: It is the daily command center, so it earns a dedicated view for clarity.

Meal Plan

  • What it does: Builds a week of dinners and a smart grocery list that reuses staples.
  • Why it exists: Meals are a constant stressor; solving them removes a recurring cognitive burden.
  • Value: Saves time, money, and last-minute scrambling with AI-backed planning.
  • Screen value: It is a high-impact domain task, warranting its own space for clarity and trust.

Family

  • What it does: Distributes tasks across household members with clear ownership and suggestions.
  • Why it exists: Shared load without clarity causes resentment; this makes delegation visible and fair.
  • Value: Reduces bottlenecks and keeps everyone aligned on who owns what.
  • Screen value: Collaboration requires a dedicated view to prevent hidden, silent work.
